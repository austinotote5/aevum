# AEVUM Clinical Claims Boundary Matrix

## Objective
Define what AEVUM can and cannot claim to avoid regulatory overreach and buyer confusion.

## Intended Use Statement
AEVUM is a health intelligence and protocol support platform that uses user-provided and wearable-derived data to generate safety-aware daily guidance. It supports wellness and performance workflows and does not replace licensed clinical diagnosis or treatment.

## Allowed Claims
1. "Supports daily readiness and recovery decision workflows."
2. "Provides safety-aware protocol guidance using contraindication rules."
3. "Tracks adherence, trend movement, and operational health metrics."
4. "Enables audit-friendly compliance and workflow evidence export."
5. "Assists clinicians/coaches with structured monitoring and signoff workflows."

## Restricted Claims
1. "Diagnoses medical conditions."
2. "Prevents, treats, or cures diseases."
3. "Guarantees clinical outcomes."
4. "Acts as a substitute for licensed medical judgment."
5. "Is FDA-cleared/approved" (unless and until formally achieved and documented).

## Marketing Guardrails
1. Always include the clinical disclaimer in public-facing pilot and sales materials.
2. Use language such as "supports", "assists", "informs", "helps monitor".
3. Avoid deterministic language such as "will cure", "will prevent", "clinically proven" without evidence.
4. Map every external claim to one internal evidence artifact.

## Clinical Disclaimer (Short Form)
AEVUM provides informational and workflow support outputs and should be interpreted alongside licensed clinical judgment. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.

## Approval Workflow
1. Product drafts claim text.
2. Clinical lead reviews for accuracy and safety.
3. Legal/compliance approves for external use.
4. Final text version logged in `regulatory_artifacts`.
5. If claim touches risk/safety workflow, verify alignment with `docs/SAFETY_ESCALATION_POLICY.md`.
